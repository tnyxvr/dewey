How it works

Two steps.
Then you're done.

screenshots/onboarding.png

Step 01

Set your destinations once

Tell Dewey where photos and videos should go. Got a Lightroom or Final Cut project folder? Set it as the default. You can always override it per session.

screenshots/organise.png

Step 02

Plug in and confirm

Dewey sits quietly in your menu bar. When a drive is detected it taps you on the shoulder. Choose what to copy, how to handle duplicates, and hit Start.

What's included

Built for how
photographers actually work.

Lives in the menu bar

Dewey stays out of your way until it's needed. No dock icon, no windows cluttering your workspace.

RAW + video aware

Supports CR3, CR2, NEF, ARW, RAF, DNG and more. Videos and photos route to separate destinations automatically.

Smart date grouping

Too few shots for a full day folder? Dewey automatically groups by week or month so nothing gets buried.

Multi-card shoots

Shooting with a partner? Enable batch mode and each source gets its own subfolder inside the shared date folder.

Duplicate protection

Skip, replace, or keep both — you decide what happens when a file already exists at the destination.

Date range filter

Only want files from a specific shoot? Set a start and end date and Dewey ignores everything outside that window.
